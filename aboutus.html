About Swat

Swat Valley, known as the "Switzerland of the East", is famous for its breathtaking landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and historical significance. Located in northern Pakistan, it plays a vital role in tourism and education.

The valley features lush green forests, running streams, meadows, and the river Swat that originates from the Ushu mountains. Swat connects with Chitral, Upper Dir, Lower Dir, Gilgit-Baltistan, Kohistan, Buner, and Shangla.

Due to its fertile land and natural beauty, Queen Elizabeth II once referred to Swat as "the Switzerland of the East". The people of Swat are known for their hospitality and warm culture.

History of the University

The University of Swat, a public-sector institution, was announced on May 29, 2010, by the Prime Minister during his visit to the valley. It became functional in July 2010 by the order of the Governor of Khyber Pakhtunkhwa.

Dr. Muhammad Farooq Khan (Shaheed) was appointed the first Vice Chancellor in August 2010. Since then, the university has grown from 7 to 27 departments, and has established campuses for women and in Shangla.

The main campus is located at Charbagh, Swat, with ongoing development of infrastructure, academic programs, and research initiatives.
