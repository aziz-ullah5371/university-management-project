Youth Development Centre (YDC)

Empowering Students through Education, Employment & Engagement

About

Since its inception in 2002, the Higher Education Commission (HEC) of Pakistan has prioritized the development of human capital by facilitating the creation of world-class centers of excellence across various disciplines.

In 2020, HEC introduced undergraduate reforms emphasizing internships, practical learning labs, student clubs, and societies. These reforms led to the establishment of Youth Development Centers (YDCs) in major public sector universities to unify student services under one roof.

Each YDC comprises wings focused on Education, Employment, and Engagement, supporting holistic student development.

Vision

To be a vibrant center that empowers students with knowledge, skills, and experiences to become responsible citizens and future leaders through academic and extracurricular growth.

Mission

To provide a platform for students that fosters educational support, internship opportunities, practical engagement, and career development in alignment with national education policies.

Objectives

  • Integrate academic learning with real-world experiences.
  • Support student career planning and job placement.
  • Promote student involvement through clubs and societies.
  • Facilitate internships and professional exposure.
  • Provide a unified access point for student services.

Outcomes

  • Improved employability and professional readiness.
  • Enhanced academic performance and practical skills.
  • Increased participation in student-led initiatives.
  • Stronger sense of community and social responsibility.

About Us

University of Swat was established for providing higher education to the people of Swat Valley specifically and the whole of the country generally.

Since its establishment in July 2010, the university has made tremendous progress in academics, research, and infrastructure. Starting with only 7 disciplines, it now offers more than 28 programs.
